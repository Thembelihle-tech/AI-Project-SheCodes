Photosynthesis is the vital process where plants, algae, and some bacteria use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to create their own food (sugars/glucose) for energy and growth,
releasing oxygen as a byproduct, which is essential for most life on Earth.
This occurs in chloroplasts, using the green pigment chlorophyll to capture light energy,
converting it into chemical energy that fuels the creation of carbohydrates from simple inorganic molecules.
